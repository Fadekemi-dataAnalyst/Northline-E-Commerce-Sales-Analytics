Business Insights

1. Customer 1 is the most valuable customer.
Customer 1 generated the highest total revenue of $267.88 and placed 3 orders, making them the company's highest-value customer. Retaining this customer through loyalty rewards or personalized offers could help maximize future revenue.

2. Several customers have made only one purchase.
Seven out of the ten customers placed only one order, contributing significantly less revenue than repeat customers. This suggests an opportunity to improve customer retention through follow-up marketing campaigns or repeat-purchase incentives.

3. High-spending customers are not necessarily frequent buyers.
Customer 8 spent $259.98 from only one order, while Customer 1 spent $267.88 across three orders. This shows that both large one-time purchases and repeat purchases contribute to revenue, so the business should target both customer segments with different marketing strategies.

4. Two customers have been identified as at risk.
Customers 1 and 2 made purchases within their first 60 days as customers but have not placed another order in over 90 days. These customers were initially engaged but may now be inactive, making them good candidates for re-engagement campaigns or special promotional offers.

5. Customer recency indicates many inactive customers.
The recency values show that all customers have gone a long time without making another purchase, suggesting that the current customer base has become inactive. Re-engagement strategies such as email campaigns, discounts, or loyalty programs could help increase repeat purchases.
